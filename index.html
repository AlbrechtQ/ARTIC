ARTIC Collection Explorer: discover artworks by dimension/canvas ratio.

This app was created as a lightweight alternative to traditional ways of browsing major artworks of the Art Institute of Chicago. By gently dragging the sides of ARTIC's logo on the upper left corner of the page, users can explore a variety of the collection's artworks.

Since ratio is the only property taken into account when sorting artworks, this tool allows for a surprising mix of genres, styles and historical periods to appear on the page. Such chaotic approach to browsing ARTIC's artworks wants to simulate the experience of a visitor taking a casual walk through the museum's premises where all masterpieces of the collection have been shuffled, and reorganized according to ratio.


This user interface lets the viewer actively partecipate in the artworks' sorting action, making the experience a bit more engaging. The icons on the right side allow for either extra shuffling, returning to the original square format and finally inverting ratio (ie exchanging width with height), in order to browse inverted artworks of a specific format.

FAQs.

How many artworks can I browse through with this tool? Why can I not see all the artworks?

Although the Art Institute of Chicago is home to more than 300,000 artworks, this tool allows you to browse through only around 400 works which are considered major and most representative. The choice to display only selected works was made to allow users to explore and enjoy the collection's icons without the overall experience being 'diluted' by the very high number of items the institution owns. Therefore, this tool is not intended as a comprehensive way to browse the full collection. For such purpose, please visit ARTIC's official website here.


How did you choose what major artwork to include?

All artworks which you can explore here were selected as major artworks by the curators of the institute themselves. These works were marked as 'boosted' in the Art Institute of Chicago API, meaning that they were considered more important/representative compared to other artworks.


Does the ratio of the logo/searchbox exactly corresponds to the ratio of the artworks being shown?

The ratio is very close to the paintings/sculpture being shown but it's not 100% indentycal. In order to guarantee a good number of artworks being shown in each search, it was preferred to show artworks in a close range to that ratio rather than exactly 1:1 with the selected ratio.


The logo/searchbox occasionally does not seem to trigger any search, why does this happen?

The logo/searchbox triggers a search every 1/10 of a second. This is to prevent too much 'shuffling' of artworks while the search box is being resized, which could be distressing to the viewer. For this reason, very quick and abrupt drags of the sides of the box could cause the search to slightly lag behind. In case this happens, just click the refresh icon on the right side. For a smoother experience, it is advised to slowly and gently drag the sides of the search box and allow artworks to come up naturally.
